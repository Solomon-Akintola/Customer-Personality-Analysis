Introduction¶

The purpose of this analysis is to perform a comprehensive customer personality analysis using a dataset from a marketing campaign. This involves understanding the customer demographics, behavior, and response to marketing efforts. The analysis includes data preparation, exploratory data analysis (EDA), customer segmentation using clustering, and predictive modeling to predict customer responses to campaigns.

Data Preparation¶

The dataset contains various attributes related to customer demographics, purchase behavior, and responses to past marketing campaigns. Key steps in data preparation included:

  1. Loading and examining the dataset to understand the structure and missing values.
  2. Calculating age based on the year of birth.
  3. Creating a total children feature by combining the number of kids and teenagers in the household.
  4. Handling missing values by filling them with the mean of the respective columns.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)¶

  1. Age Distribution

The age distribution shows that most customers are between 30 and 70 years old, with a peak around 50 years. This indicates that the customer base is primarily middle-aged, which can influence marketing strategies and product offerings to suit this age group.

  1. Income Distribution

    The income distribution is slightly right-skewed, with most customers earning between $20,000 and $80,000. A smaller group earns significantly higher, indicating a potential segment for premium products and services.

  2. Marital Status Distribution

    The majority of customers are either married or living together, followed by single customers. This information can be useful for tailoring marketing messages and products to family-oriented customers.

  3. Education Level Distribution

    Most customers have a graduation level of education, followed by those with a PhD. This indicates a relatively educated customer base, which might be more receptive to detailed product information and sophisticated marketing campaigns.

Customer Segmentation¶

Using the KMeans clustering algorithm, we segmented customers into four clusters based on their demographics and purchasing behavior. The optimal number of clusters was determined using the elbow method.

  • Cluster 0: Represents high-income customers with a high number of purchases across different product categories.
  • Cluster 1: Represents middle-aged, middle-income customers with moderate purchasing behavior.
  • Cluster 2: Represents younger customers with lower income and fewer purchases.
  • Cluster 3: Represents older customers with varied income levels and purchasing behavior.

Predictive Modeling¶

A logistic regression model was used to predict customer responses to marketing campaigns. The model achieved a reasonable accuracy, indicating its effectiveness in predicting customer responses based on their demographics and past behavior.

  • Confusion Matrix: Shows the model's performance in correctly predicting positive and negative responses.
  • Classification Report: Provides precision, recall, and F1-score for the model, indicating its overall effectiveness.
  • Accuracy Score: The accuracy score provides a single metric to evaluate the model's performance.

Business Implications¶

  1. Targeted Marketing: Understanding the age and income distribution helps in creating targeted marketing campaigns. For example, middle-aged customers with higher income can be targeted for premium products.
  2. Product Development: Insights from customer segmentation can inform product development. For example, products catering to families and middle-aged customers can be prioritized.
  3. Customer Retention: Predictive modeling helps in identifying customers likely to respond positively to campaigns, enabling better allocation of marketing resources and improving customer retention strategies.
  4. Personalized Communication: Knowing the education level and marital status allows for more personalized communication, increasing the likelihood of engagement and conversion.
